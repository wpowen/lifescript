# Obsidian Story Seal

## Design Philosophy

The new icon shifts from an "ancient sigil" metaphor to an Apple-style premium object: a single, unmistakable mark that feels engineered, polished, and expensive. The design is built around one centered story emblem inside a glass-metal medallion, so the icon reads instantly at small sizes and still carries presence on a large App Store canvas. The goal is not ornament, but authority through restraint.

Form follows clarity. The silhouette is intentionally simple: a circular seal, a vertical spine, and two opening wings that imply both an open book and a story gateway. That dual reading matters. Users should feel that this app is not just for reading content, but for stepping into and directing a narrative world. Every major shape is broad, smooth, and legible so the icon never turns muddy when reduced to home-screen scale.

Materiality carries the sense of luxury. The background uses obsidian blacks with very low-contrast cool and warm light blooms, while the central seal uses champagne-gold metal and a soft glass highlight. This pulls the icon closer to Apple's recent visual language: disciplined contrast, layered depth, and subtle optical softness instead of loud gradients or decorative texture. The finish should feel machined and luminous, as if the icon were a premium object photographed under studio light.

Composition is deliberately centered and calm. Apple-style icons generally work best when they establish one focal object with a clear depth stack, rather than many competing details. The outer halo, metallic ring, inner gloss, and symbol shadow all exist to reinforce that hierarchy. Nothing should read as accidental. The viewer sees a dark field first, then the medallion, then the story mark, in a clean sequence.

This implementation is currently delivered as a flattened 1024x1024 PNG so it fits the existing Xcode asset pipeline. Even without Apple's layered Icon Composer workflow, the design should still preserve the key principles: immediate recognition, premium material cues, and a refined visual weight that makes the product feel more high-end the moment it appears on the home screen.
